How does it economically affect our country?

The economic contributions of immigrants after the 1965 Act have been proven to be enormous and increasingly vital to America. According to the U.S. Department of Labor's most recent study, today immigrants make up 16.9 percent of the U.S. labor force. In 2014, they earned $1.3 trillion and contributed $105 billion in state and local taxes and $224 billion in federal taxes. In fact, even undocumented immigrants contributed more than $11.6 billion in state and local taxes because, despite being undocumented, they still pay property taxes, sales tax, and pay income tax through a 1-10 income tax return form. Currently, immigrants are participating at a slightly higher rate than Americans in the U.S. workforce, with 73.4 percent of foreign-born workers holding jobs, compared to 71.1 percent of native-born workers. [1] What is note worthy is the buried potential of the immigrants. The Immigrants have a over all rate of starting businesses 2 times higher than native born citizens. These fact demonstrates how immigrants is an essential driver of the American Economic growth.
